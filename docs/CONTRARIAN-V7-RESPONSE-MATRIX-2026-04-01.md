## What This Is

This document does **not** replace the Contrarian skill.

The Contrarian skill is the attacker.
This matrix is the founder's defense board.

Its job is to stop three bad loops:

1. Re-arguing objections that have already been answered in doctrine
2. Confusing messaging problems with product problems
3. Expecting paper arguments to solve problems that only users can solve

The operating rule is simple:

- If an objection is solved in doctrine, the next Contrarian pass must attack the **new** doctrine, not the retired version.
- If an objection is a product or ops issue, we do not keep rewriting positioning to solve it.
- If an objection requires live proof, we stop pretending another paragraph will settle it.

---

## Status Labels

- **Resolved in doctrine**: the old objection no longer lands against the current plan
- **Partially resolved**: the direction is better, but there is still a real vulnerability
- **Open**: still a live strategic or execution problem
- **Evidence only**: can only be closed by real users and real data

## Bucketed View

### Closed on Paper

These should no longer score as if nothing changed:

- Peloton analogy / hardware transfer claim
- Filtering-as-homepage posture
- ELITE as current promise
- Squad model being structurally absent

### Not Closed Until We Build / Operate Better

These still require product or launch decisions:

- Email deliverability and pulse reliability
- Founder load from human layer
- Feed density / ghost-town risk
- Exact operational shape of Sprint 4

### Not Closed Until Users Exist

These are evidence questions, not writing questions:

- Can DMs convert to paid ENTER users?
- Can users maintain the streak beyond Week 2?
- Does the pulse remain habit-forming through Day 40?
- Do enough operators hit 70+ to make PROVEN real?

## What The Next Contrarian Pass Must Judge

The next pass should attack the **current** model, not the retired one.

That means the real live questions are:

1. Does the single-experience doctrine hold up without the Peloton analogy?
2. Is the human layer light enough to survive Sprint 4?
3. Does the early social layer create accountability or ghost-town doubt?
4. Is the score ladder believable and motivating at `30 / 70 / 90`?
5. Does the broad-message / operator-message / elite-message hierarchy feel coherent or schizophrenic?
6. What remains impossible to settle without users?

If the Contrarian keeps winning on arguments we explicitly retired, the pass is outdated.
If the Contrarian wins on live-evidence gaps, that is fair.
