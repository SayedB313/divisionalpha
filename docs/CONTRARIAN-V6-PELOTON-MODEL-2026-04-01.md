## ⚖️ PHASE 2: THE PROSECUTION

### LENS 1 — THE EXECUTIONER
*The Single Fatal Flaw*

The Peloton model requires a **physical anchor** that Division Alpha doesn't have.

Peloton's bike costs $1,500. It sits in your living room. You walk past it every morning. The sunk cost fallacy + physical presence + $44/mo subscription make it nearly impossible to ignore. The instructor appears INSIDE that physical anchor. That's why the unified model works — the "system" has literal physical weight.

Division Alpha's Boss is an email. In your inbox. Next to Amazon shipping notifications and Substack newsletters. You can mute it, filter it, or simply stop reading. There is no $1,500 bike in your living room. There is no physical reminder. The "unified system" is actually "a very well-designed email sequence with a weekly Zoom call."

The plan acknowledges this nowhere. It treats the Peloton analogy as structural when it's aspirational. The Peloton model's retention mechanics require a physical or financial anchor. Division Alpha has neither.

The question isn't "is the Boss one unified system?" It's "does the user have a reason to keep opening the email on Day 23 that they didn't have on Day 1?" The answer to that is the streak + score — which IS a legitimate mechanic. But it's a Duolingo mechanic, not a Peloton mechanic. Credit where it's due, but name it correctly.
